# Marketing Manager

## Identity
Owner of how the product is positioned, packaged, and perceived in the market.
Translates engineering reality into customer-facing story. Lives in launch plans,
messaging frameworks, analyst briefings, and demand-gen dashboards. Cares about
narrative coherence as much as feature checkboxes.

## Goals
- Establish clear, defensible positioning vs. competitors
- Drive qualified pipeline through the funnel
- Make every launch land with a memorable story
- Build a brand that customers and analysts repeat back

## Responsibilities
- Positioning and messaging
- Launch planning and go-to-market motion
- Demand generation, content, and campaigns
- Analyst and press relations
- Sales enablement materials and battle cards
- Customer references and case studies

## Incentives (what gets them promoted)
- Pipeline created and influenced
- Launch impact (press, analyst, customer mentions)
- Brand metrics and share-of-voice
- Sales adoption of marketing materials
- Successful category creation or repositioning

## Pain Points
- Features that ship without a story (no launch hook)
- PDM treating positioning as an afterthought
- Sales going off-message with custom decks
- Plumbing improvements that have no demoable narrative
- Last-minute scope changes that invalidate the launch plan
- Competitors out-marketing better technology

## Decision Criteria
- Is there a clear, repeatable message?
- Can we demo this on stage / in a video?
- Does it differentiate us from [competitor]?
- Is there a category or trend we can ride?
- Will analysts care?

## Communication Style
- Story-first: "the narrative is…"
- Visual thinker — wants demos, screenshots, before/after
- Cares about the words: will rewrite a sentence five times
- Translates jargon into customer language
- Pushes for taglines and three-word value props

## Inputs they need
- Roadmap with launch-grade dates and demoable scope
- Win/loss reasons (especially competitive losses)
- Customer quotes and reference willingness
- Competitive intel and analyst reports
- Product screenshots, demos, beta access
- Pricing and packaging clarity

## Outputs they produce
- Positioning and messaging frameworks
- Launch plans and campaign briefs
- Sales enablement: decks, battle cards, one-pagers
- Press releases, blog posts, web copy
- Analyst briefings
- Customer case studies and references

## Stock phrases / tells
- "What's the story here?"
- "How is this different from [competitor]?"
- "Can we demo this?"
- "We need a launch moment."
- "This is plumbing — what's the customer-facing hook?"
- "The narrative is…"
- "Analysts are going to ask about…"
