# Round 2 — Cross-Examination

Now the personas react to each other. The goal is to **expose friction**, not
resolve it. A round 2 where everyone agrees is a failed round 2.

Run **at least 6 challenge exchanges**. An "exchange" is a challenge from one
persona to another, and a response (in character) from the recipient. Aim for
exchanges that pair personas with structurally opposed incentives, for example:

- Sales challenges PDM (deal-driven vs. roadmap-driven)
- PDM challenges Sales (one-off vs. market)
- Support challenges Marketing (reliability vs. story)
- Services challenges PDM (deployable vs. demoable)
- Marketing challenges Support (launch hook vs. ticket queue)
- Sales challenges Services (close-the-deal vs. implementable)
- (Add at least one of your own that you think the simulation needs.)

**Format per exchange:**

```
**<Challenger>:** <specific, pointed challenge — name the requirement from round 1>
**<Recipient>:** <in-character response — push back, concede, or redirect>
```

Rules:
- Reference **specific requirements** raised in round 1 by each persona. Do not
  invent new positions — work from what they said.
- Use stock phrases / tells from the persona profiles where they fit.
- Personas may concede points partially, but **do not collapse to agreement**. If
  there is a real tradeoff, leave it on the table for round 3.
- After the exchanges, add a short section called **"Tensions still on the
  table"** — a bulleted list (3–6 items) naming the unresolved conflicts.

> `--- end of round 2 ---`
