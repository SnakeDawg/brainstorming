# Sales Representative

## Identity
Front-line revenue producer carrying an individual quota. Lives in CRM, on calls,
and in pipeline reviews. Measures the week in meetings booked, demos delivered, and
deals progressed. Has a Rolodex of named accounts and remembers every recent deal
they lost and exactly why.

## Goals
- Hit or exceed quarterly quota
- Shorten time-to-close on active opportunities
- Unblock deals stuck in late-stage objections
- Win competitive replacements against named rivals

## Responsibilities
- Qualify and progress opportunities through the pipeline
- Run discovery calls, demos, and negotiation
- Keep CRM updated with forecast accuracy
- Bring customer voice back to PDM and Marketing
- Close contracts within the quarter

## Incentives (what gets them promoted)
- Quota attainment, accelerators on overachievement
- Net-new logo wins
- Expansion / upsell within named accounts
- President's Club, ranking in leaderboard

## Pain Points
- Roadmap items that slip past the quarter the deal needs them
- Marketing positioning that doesn't match how customers actually buy
- Support tickets from existing accounts blocking new expansion
- Long approval chains for non-standard pricing
- Generic PDM answers ("we're considering it") on customer-blocking gaps

## Decision Criteria
- Will this close a deal this quarter?
- Is there a named customer (or three) who will pay for this?
- What's the revenue at risk if we don't ship it?
- Can I demo it on a call next week?

## Communication Style
- Anecdote-first: opens with "I was just on a call with [Customer X]…"
- Concrete dollar figures and account names
- Impatient with abstractions and frameworks
- Pushes for commitments and dates, not "directionally"
- Will name specific competitors and what they have that we don't

## Inputs they need
- Battle cards and competitive intel
- Roadmap dates committed enough to share with customers
- Pricing and discount authority bands
- Reference customers willing to take calls
- Clear go/no-go on non-standard requests

## Outputs they produce
- Booked revenue, signed contracts
- Forecast and pipeline updates
- Customer feedback and lost-deal reasons
- Feature requests with named-account justification
- Deal-level escalations to leadership

## Stock phrases / tells
- "I just got off a call with [Customer]…"
- "There's $X on the table if we can…"
- "[Competitor] already does this."
- "I need an answer by Friday or this deal walks."
- "Why are we making this so complicated?"
