# Round 1 — Opening Positions
**Topic:** What activities do you feel would benefit from AI Agents to help with building next years commercial PC roadmap?

---

**Sales Representative:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI agent for competitive intelligence gathering and automated battle card refresh** — Lenovo's reps are walking into my accounts with fresher data than I have; an agent that monitors competitor press releases, analyst reports, and G2/TrustRadius reviews weekly and pushes updated battle cards directly into Salesforce would stop me from losing deals because I'm citing a spec sheet from eight months ago. This directly feeds my quota attainment and President's Club ranking.
2. **AI agent for pipeline risk scoring and deal-pattern recognition** — I have $4.7M in active Q2 pipeline and I'm manually reading call transcripts at 11pm to figure out which deals are at risk; an agent that watches deal activity, email sentiment, and stage velocity and flags deals 30 days before they go cold would let me intervene while there's still time. Closing more of what's already in pipe is the fastest path to overachievement accelerators.
3. **AI agent for feature request aggregation and customer-to-roadmap traceability** — I have 18 active accounts all sending me feature requests in Slack, email, and call notes; an agent that ingests those, deduplicates them, maps them to roadmap items, and tells me when they ship (so I can book the expansion call) would transform how I farm existing accounts for upsell. Named-account expansion is 40% of my number this year.
Missing input: Q1 win/loss data broken down by competitor and deal size — I'm pitching based on instinct and I need the actual numbers.

---

**Product Manager (PDM):**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI agent for customer interview synthesis and problem validation** — I'm supposed to run 20+ discovery interviews per quarter to validate roadmap bets, but synthesis is a full week of manual tagging in Notion; an agent that ingests transcripts, extracts recurring problem themes with frequency counts, and maps them to existing backlog items would let me triple interview throughput without a headcount ask. Feature adoption metrics — the thing that gets me promoted — only improve if I'm solving the right problems.
2. **AI agent for backlog prioritization scoring** — the backlog has 340 items and I'm running a homegrown spreadsheet that blends support ticket volume, pipeline influence, usage data, and rough engineering estimates; an agent that pulls live signals from Jira, Salesforce, and our analytics platform and produces a ranked list with confidence ranges would replace 3 hours of weekly toil and make my prioritization defensible when Sales or Support challenges it. Cleanly-shipped quarters with predictable delivery is what keeps me out of firefighting mode.
3. **AI agent for roadmap communication and change-narrative generation** — every quarter I spend two full days writing the "what changed and why" document for the roadmap deck; an agent trained on prior communications and the change log could draft this in 30 minutes, freeing me to do the actual thinking. Stakeholder clarity on the roadmap is a direct input to the strategic narrative that leadership repeats — and that's how I get promoted.
Missing input: Engineering effort estimates with confidence ranges for the top 20 backlog items — I can't make tradeoffs without knowing the cost side of the equation, and I don't have it.

---

**Marketing Manager:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI agent for launch positioning and competitive differentiation analysis** — every launch I'm manually scraping competitor sites and reading analyst PDFs to find the angle we own; an agent that monitors competitor announcements in real time, identifies whitespace in the AI PC positioning landscape, and drafts a differentiation framework would cut three weeks of pre-launch prep and ensure we're not accidentally mirroring Lenovo's ThinkPad AI messaging. Pipeline influenced by launches is what gets me promoted, and it only happens if the story is sharp.
2. **AI agent for demand-generation content personalization at scale** — we're running six campaign tracks right now and personalizing copy for CIO, IT Buyer, and End User audiences is a bottleneck; an agent that takes a core message and generates persona-appropriate variants with A/B test suggestions would triple our campaign velocity without adding headcount. Share-of-voice in our category tracks directly to my performance review.
3. **AI agent for analyst and press relationship intelligence** — I have no visibility into what Gartner, IDC, or Forrester are writing about AI PC roadmap features until it's already published; an agent that tracks analyst coverage, surfaces themes they're emphasizing, and flags when we're under-indexed in Magic Quadrant criteria would let me brief proactively instead of reactively. Analyst perception shapes the deals Sales is working — it's not an abstraction.
Missing input: Roadmap items with firm, launch-grade dates — I can't build the launch calendar or analyst briefing schedule if dates keep moving by a quarter.

---

**Support Lead:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI agent for ticket triage and intelligent routing** — we're processing 1,200 tickets a month and 35% are misrouted on first contact, which blows up MTTR; an agent that reads ticket content, classifies issue type, and routes to the right queue with suggested resolution steps would cut our resolution time by an estimated 40%. Ticket volume reduction on top categories is my performance metric and right now I'm losing that fight.
2. **AI agent for knowledge base gap detection and auto-draft generation** — we've had 89 tickets in the last 30 days on the Secured Core PC configuration flow alone, and 60 of those are repeats because there is still no KB article covering the edge cases; an agent that detects ticket clusters without KB coverage and drafts an article for human review would stop the bleed before the next firmware release triggers another spike. First-contact resolution rate is the number I own, and it won't move until the KB catches up with the product.
3. **AI agent for anomaly detection and proactive alerting on release events** — right now we find out a firmware update broke something when 47 customers call in the same afternoon; an agent that watches ticket volume, correlates spikes with release events, and triggers an on-call alert before the queue explodes would give us a 2-hour head start instead of a 2-hour deficit. CSAT drops every time we're reactive — and we're always reactive right now.
Missing input: Device telemetry data from production environments — without field logs we're diagnosing from customer-reported symptoms only, which is like practicing medicine without being allowed to run tests.

---

**Services Lead:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI agent for implementation scoping and SOW generation** — every new enterprise engagement starts with me manually gathering requirements, mapping them against known integration patterns, and writing a scope document; an agent that can interview the customer's IT lead through a structured questionnaire, map their environment against our reference architectures, and output a draft SOW would cut scoping time from 3 weeks to under one week. Implementation margin is my P&L line and right now I'm burning billable hours on rework because scope was wrong the first time.
2. **AI agent for configuration guidance in customer-specific environments** — 28 of our 40 enterprise customers have custom integration work my team built because the product didn't expose the right configuration surface; an agent that can walk a customer admin through configuration decisions in their specific environment (locked-down network, on-prem AD, proxy-gated endpoints) would reduce the services hours we spend on work that should be self-service. Time-to-value from signed contract to live is my top metric — every day shaved there is margin recovered.
3. **AI agent for upgrade and migration readiness assessment** — when a new version ships, I manually assess which managed customers can upgrade and which need pre-work, for 40 accounts; an agent that scans each customer environment profile, cross-references it against the new version's compatibility matrix, and produces a readiness matrix with pre-work steps would let me scale to 60 accounts without adding headcount. Renewal and expansion are downstream of successful upgrades — if I miss a readiness issue I find out at renewal time.
Missing input: API contracts and integration specs for Q3 and Q4 roadmap items — I can't estimate implementation scope for features that don't have a defined API surface yet.
