# Round 1 — Opening Positions
**Scenario:** cross_functional_workshop
**Topic:** What activities do you feel would benefit from AI Agents to help with building next years commercial pc roadmap?
**Run:** 2026-04-26-1445

---

**Sales Representative:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI Agent for real-time competitive deal intelligence** — a curated feed of what Lenovo, Dell, and HP are announcing in the commercial PC segment, surfaced in CRM before customer calls — If I can arm reps with competitive intel the morning of a call, my close rate goes up and I hit accelerator.
2. **AI Agent to synthesize customer win/loss signals into roadmap requirements** — right now I'm the only one who knows why we lost Hartwell Bank to Dell last quarter; that reason should be captured automatically and turned into a requirement brief, not buried in a Salesforce note — Named-account win attribution is what drives expansion revenue.
3. **AI Agent for late-stage deal roadmap gap alerts** — when a deal enters late stage and the customer has a requirement we can't meet today, I need an agent that surfaces the nearest roadmap item and a commit date I can share — Unblocking late-stage deals with committed dates is where my accelerator lives.

Missing input: Win/loss data from the last two quarters mapped to specific product gaps — I have anecdotes, not analysis.

---

**Product Manager (PDM):**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI Agent to aggregate and score customer feedback signals across channels** — CRM notes, support tickets, usage telemetry, and NPS verbatims ingested and ranked weekly into the top 10 market problems, with volume and segment breakdown — If I can prove adoption-metric attribution to roadmap decisions, that's the narrative that gets me to the leadership table.
2. **AI Agent for roadmap prioritization assistance** — given a candidate requirement, the agent pulls historical effort estimates, segment fit, and win/loss correlation and produces an auditable scorecard — Clean, evidence-backed quarters with predictable delivery are what get me promoted.
3. **AI Agent to detect and flag scope creep in real-time** — as requirements land in the backlog, the agent compares them against current roadmap scope and flags "this displaces item X; estimated delivery impact: N weeks" — If engineering surprises stop blowing up my quarters mid-flight, I look like I'm in control of the roadmap.

Missing input: Engineering effort estimates with confidence ranges for the top 20 backlog items — the prioritization agent is meaningless without cost ground truth.

---

**Marketing Manager:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI Agent for continuous competitive narrative monitoring** — a daily digest scanning competitor announcements, Gartner and IDC reports, and press coverage in the commercial PC segment, flagging where our current positioning has new gaps or new ammunition — Share-of-voice improvements and analyst-facing narrative drive my launch impact numbers.
2. **AI Agent to draft launch positioning from product specs** — when a roadmap item reaches launch-ready, the agent reads the PRD and auto-generates a first-draft positioning framework: headline, three differentiators, the analyst question it answers — Launch speed and narrative coherence are on my scorecard.
3. **AI Agent for customer reference and case study pipeline matching** — given a roadmap item, the agent surfaces which existing customers have the use case, their CSAT score, and whether they've been flagged as reference-willing — Reference customers anchoring a launch drive analyst credibility and press pickup.

Missing input: Roadmap dates with launch-grade confidence — I can't build a campaign against items that slip every quarter.

---

**Support Lead:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI Agent for automated ticket trend detection and roadmap escalation** — the agent ingests the ServiceNow queue and identifies when any issue type exceeds 15 tickets in 7 days, auto-generates a defect brief, and creates a tagged roadmap escalation for PDM's backlog — Ticket volume reduction on top categories is literally my performance metric.
2. **AI Agent to generate and maintain the knowledge base from resolved tickets** — as tickets close, the agent drafts a KB article from the resolution thread, flags it for human review, and publishes on approval; we had 214 KB articles expire last year — First-contact resolution rate is what I'm measured on.
3. **AI Agent for customer self-service roadmap status** — before a customer submits a ticket, an agent checks whether the issue maps to a known defect or a planned roadmap item and returns status; the "when is this getting fixed" category is 31% of current volume — Deflection and CSAT improvement are how I get promoted.

Missing input: Defect prioritization SLA from PDM — without committed timelines on the top 10 open defects, the self-service agent can't give customers accurate status.

---

**Services Lead:**
Top 3 requirements:
1. **AI Agent to generate implementation plans from product specs** — when a new product or feature is confirmed, the agent reads the PRD and generates a first-cut implementation plan: phases, dependencies, environment requirements, and estimated time-to-value — Time-to-value is my north star metric.
2. **AI Agent for customer environment configuration drift detection** — we have 47 active managed accounts; the agent monitors configuration state and flags when a customer's setup has drifted from certified baseline; Brinley Health spent three weeks in a support loop last quarter because of an undocumented config change — Implementation margin improves when I'm not re-diagnosing drift on every engagement.
3. **AI Agent for deployment runbook generation and validation** — the agent takes a product update and auto-drafts a runbook: pre-checks, rollback steps, environment-specific flags; Services has been writing these manually for every release — Reusable assets contributed back to the practice are an explicit promotion criterion.

Missing input: API specs and integration contracts for the H2 roadmap items — I can't scope implementations or train the planning agent without knowing what the APIs look like.

--- end of round 1 ---
